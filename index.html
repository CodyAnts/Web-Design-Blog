Hey there! I'm Cody, and this blog is about me learning to build webpages with code.

8th September, 2020

As someone who is studying to hopefully one day become a user experience designer, I figured I'd better at least learn the basics of web development. "It can't be that hard, right?"
I innocently thought to myself. Boy, was I wrong. The sheer amount of syntax, different terms and countless ways to complete the same task was overwhelming to me.

My brain doesn't seem to work in a "line by line" programming way so it was very difficult for me to learn a concept, then immediately after complete a task using what I'd just learned because I always felt I was doing it wrong, or there was something I was missing. Slowly I realised that I needed to give myself time for the concepts to sink in, and I needed dedicated blocks of time with no distractions to focus if I wanted to achieve anything in this class. Once I had figured that out, things started to fall into place... slowly.
